The Be'er Sheva fringe-fingered lizard ("Acanthodactylus beershebensis") is a species of lizard in the Lacertidae family. It is a member of the subgenus Lacertinae, and the tribe Acanthodactylus (spiny footed lizards). Considered a separate species based on morphological distinction, and isolated location; it shares a large portion of its genetics with "Acanthodactylus pardalis" in this genus. Many of the individual species under this genus are similar, but varying coloration explains why each species have been separated. Like all "Acanthodactylus", the beershebensis lays eggs, varying from three to seven eggs at a time. Adults vary in size from 17 to 20 centimeters, but can get much larger. It is endemic to the loess scrublands of the Negev desert in Israel and the Palestinian Territories, a biodiversity hotspot.
